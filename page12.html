To the Heart That Raised Me

If I could gather all the words in the world, still, none would be enough to tell you how much you mean to me.

Because how do you even begin to describe someone who gave you everything… even when she had nothing left for herself?
How do you measure the love of a mother who chose her child’s happiness over her own, every single day?

Kumusta ka, Ma?
How are you—really—beneath the smiles you wear like armor?
How are you, besides the fact that you never grow tired of taking care of us… of me?
I wonder about that sometimes.

Because I know you're the kind of woman who gives her all without asking if she has enough left for herself.
And I just hope, even once in a while, the world gives you back even a fraction of what you've poured into it.

Science says a mother carries her child for 9 months.
But I don’t believe that.
Because even now, even as I stand on my own two feet, I still feel your arms around me...
In the way I feel safe for no reason, in the quiet comfort that follows me when I’m scared, in the warmth I carry even in the coldest places.
You never stopped carrying me, Mama. You just learned how to do it without your hands.

You’ve given me more than time, more than care, more than love.
You gave me your youth, your strength, your sleep, your dreams… so I could grow, breathe, live, and become someone.
And I didn’t always see it— not until life taught me what it costs to give, to sacrifice, to hold yourself together when the world is falling apart.

There is no poetry deep enough, no song strong enough, to capture the quiet, unnoticed, everyday ways you’ve saved me.
In your silence. In your patience. In your steady, unshakable love.

You are my mother. And no title—no award, no crown—could ever compare to that.
You are God’s greatest gift to me, wrapped not in ribbons, but in courage, scars, and relentless love.

You are in every part of me.
In my smile, because you taught me how to find joy even when life is hard.
In my voice, because you listened to me when no one else did.
In my dreams, because you told me I was capable, even when I doubted myself.
In my strength, because I watched you fight battles you never spoke of.
Every step I take, every word I speak—it all echoes with the love you raised me in.

And if life gave me a thousand chances to start again, I would still choose you, Mama. Every single time.
Because even in another life, I’d still want to come from your heart.

Maybe I haven’t said it enough. Maybe I still won’t say it perfectly.
But I mean this with everything I am:

In everything I do, in every dream I chase, I carry you.
Not as a memory—but as my strength, my guide, my home.

Your love is the foundation of who I am.
It is the voice that tells me to keep going. The compass that brings me back when I feel lost.
And no matter how far life takes me… I will always come back to the heart that raised me.

I may grow older, taller, stronger—
but I will always be your child.
Always your reason. Always your miracle.
Always, your greatest proof that your love built something beautiful.
