Ma, I'm Sorry

Ma, I'm sorry.
For the moments I was quiet when I should’ve said, “I love you.”
For the times I didn’t reply… when all you wanted was to hear how I was.
For the missed calls, the unread messages, the days I let life sweep me away—while you were there, waiting.

I know it’s not just about answering a phone.
It’s about answering your heart.
And I’m sorry for the times I didn’t.

Sometimes it’s hard to explain how fast the world moves, how noise fills the spaces I should be using to talk to you—but none of that is an excuse.
Because if there’s one voice I should never let get drowned out, it’s yours.

I’m sorry for the moments I forgot how much you worry.
How every silence from me echoes louder in your chest.
How your mind rewrites quiet into fear—and still, you choose patience instead of anger.

I’m sorry for making you feel like I wasn’t thinking of you.
The truth is, I always am.
You are stitched into my every prayer, every moment I smile, every dream I chase.

And I know saying “sorry” isn’t magic.
It won’t undo the times I made you feel unheard, unneeded.
But I promise I am learning—to slow down, to show up, to answer when it matters.

Because you matter.
And I will spend forever trying to be the kind of child your love deserves.

Ma… I'm sorry for everything.

For the things I did that hurt you—even if I didn’t mean to.
For the things I didn’t do, that you silently hoped I would.
For the raised tones, the stubborn replies, the moments I walked away instead of leaning in.
For every time I made you feel like your love wasn’t enough—when in truth, it was more than I ever deserved.

I’m sorry for the burdens I added to the ones you already carried.
For the days you gave your all and I didn’t even say thank you.
For being too caught up in growing up, that I forgot you were growing tired.

I’m sorry for not always understanding.
For not seeing that behind your strength was a tired woman just trying to hold everything together.
And I’m sorry that sometimes, I didn’t make it easier for you—when all you’ve ever tried to do was make life easier for me.

Ma, for everything I said, everything I didn’t,
everything I did, and everything I failed to do—

I’m sorry.
And I love you more than words will ever be able to say.
