Dear Mama,

I just graduated from Senior High—and now, I’m standing on the edge of something even bigger: college. And while I’m proud, excited, and hopeful…I’m also scared.

Because things are about to get more complicated. Schedules tighter. Days longer. Life busier. There will be more things to do, more people to meet, and sometimes, I might forget to breathe—let alone answer a message, or call you when I should.

Ma, there will be days ahead when I might not come home early. Days when my phone stays silent—not because I forgot you, but because life got loud.

There will be moments when I’m so tired, I fall asleep without saying goodnight, or when I’m so caught up in school, in tasks, in the whirlwind of becoming…that I won’t say the things I should.

But please know this: even if I miss a few texts, even if I come home late, even if my voice gets lost in the noise of chasing my dreams—you will never stop being the center of my world.

And in those moments, I need you to remember this:

You are never unseen. You are never set aside. You are never unloved.

Even in the silence, you are with me. Even in the distance, your love is what grounds me.

Every achievement I’ll reach, every goal I’ll touch, will always have your fingerprints on it. Because I carry your sacrifices in my every step. I walk into classrooms with the strength you gave me. I face new challenges with the bravery I saw in you every day.

College will be a whole new journey. It will stretch me, change me, challenge me—but it will never erase where I came from.

And I came from you, Mama. From your tears, your laughter, your whispered prayers, your hands that worked without rest so mine could hold a diploma.

So even if I can’t always talk to you as much, please don’t think I’ve forgotten you nor am I angry at you.

I’m just working hard…so that one day, I can give you even a piece of the life you gave up for me.

Please don’t mistake my absence for neglect. Don’t let unanswered calls make you feel unwanted. It’s not that I don’t care—it’s that I’m trying to build something that you can be proud of. Something you deserve. A life that’s worthy of the sacrifices you made.

And even if I don’t say it every day, even if I fail sometimes to show it the way you deserve—You are the constant in my ever-changing world.

So whenever you start to wonder, whenever loneliness tries to whisper lies to you—remember this:

I’m still your child. Still holding your lessons. Still drawing strength from your love.

And nothing—not time, not distance, not the storms of life—can ever take that away.
